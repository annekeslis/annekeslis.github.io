Speaking is one of the most complicated motor skills humans acquire. Yet, neurobiologically healthy individuals are extremely successful in producing intelligible speech. I am interested in figuring out how speakers control articulatory movements and what underlying factors result in breakdown of these movements. More specifically, I examine how and why different types of neurodegenerative disorders, such as Parkinson's disease, ALS, and Cerebellar Ataxia, contribute to impairments at the speech motor level.
One of my long term goals is to find reliable kinematic and acoustic markers to identify and separate different types of dysarthria's caused by these neurodegenerative disorders.
